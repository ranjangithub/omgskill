# 🛡️ Insurance — AI Resources

## AI & Your Industry

Insurance is one of the most data-rich, AI-ready industries on earth — and has been using statistical models for pricing since before "machine learning" was a term. In 2026, the transformation is in what's now possible at the edges: real-time telematics underwriting, AI-driven claims automation that resolves simple property claims in under 10 minutes without human touch, and computer vision replacing field inspectors for damage assessment. The strategic pressure is regulatory — NAIC's Model Bulletin on AI use in underwriting is forcing carriers to document AI decision-making in ways that satisfy state insurance commissioners, and the EU's AI Act classifies insurance scoring as "high-risk AI." The carriers winning are those who can show regulators a governance paper trail while still deploying faster than competitors.

### What's actually changing

- AI claims processing is live at Lemonade (90-second claims), Tractable (auto damage AI used by top-10 carriers), and Clearcover — simple claims that took 2 weeks now resolve in minutes
- Computer vision for property damage assessment is replacing field inspectors for auto, property, and catastrophe claims; State Farm, Allstate, and Erie Insurance are active deployers
- Telematics-based UBI (Usage-Based Insurance) now covers 16M+ US auto policies; behavioral AI scoring is replacing demographic proxies in states where regulators have restricted proxy variables
- NAIC's 2023 Model Bulletin on AI/ML in insurance underwriting is being adopted by state regulators; actuaries are being asked to produce explainability documentation that traditional black-box models cannot provide
- Fraud detection AI (Coalition, Shift Technology, Verisk) is reducing claims fraud detection time from months to days; the ROI case is the clearest of any insurance AI application
- Parametric insurance (automatic payouts based on weather/event triggers) is growing at 30%+ annually, enabled entirely by AI weather modeling and satellite data
