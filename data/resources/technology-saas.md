# 💻 Technology / SaaS — AI Resources

## AI & Your Industry

Technology and SaaS companies are living inside the transformation every other industry is still watching from a distance. In 2026, AI coding agents (Cursor, GitHub Copilot, Claude Code, Codex) are changing what a 10-person engineering team can ship in a sprint. The real competitive pressure isn't "should we use AI" — it's "which layer of the stack do we own when AI commoditizes everything below it." LLM APIs are infrastructure, agentic systems are eating workflow software, and the companies building evaluation frameworks, guardrails, and observability tooling are quietly becoming the most defensible businesses in the ecosystem. For SaaS founders and tech leaders, the strategic question is whether your product moat survives a world where AI can replicate its core function in 6 months.

### What's actually changing

- AI coding agents are reducing first-draft development time by 40–60% in production engineering teams; the bottleneck has shifted from writing code to reviewing and testing it
- GitHub Copilot crossed 1.3M enterprise seat subscriptions in 2025; agentic coding (full feature branches, not just autocomplete) is now the default pitch from every major vendor
- LLM API costs dropped 90%+ in 24 months — GPT-4-class capability now costs under $1/million input tokens, making AI features economically viable for mid-market SaaS at scale
- RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) has become the standard enterprise AI pattern; the ecosystem around chunking, embedding, reranking, and evaluation (LangChain, LlamaIndex, Weaviate) is maturing fast
- AI observability (LangSmith, Weights & Biases, Arize) is becoming a required line item for any production AI system — the "we'll just check it manually" era is over
- The EU AI Act's tiered compliance framework directly affects SaaS products deployed to EU customers; GPAI model obligations became enforceable in August 2025
