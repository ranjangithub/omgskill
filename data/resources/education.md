# 🎓 Education — AI Resources

## AI & Your Industry

Education is experiencing its most significant structural disruption since the printing press — which is both an accurate description and an overused one. The practical reality in 2026: AI tutoring systems (Khanmigo, Synthesis, Carnegie Learning) are demonstrating measurable learning gains in controlled studies, large districts are actively deploying AI writing feedback tools, and the academic integrity question is no longer "how do we detect AI writing" but "how do we redesign assessment for a world where AI drafts are inevitable." The biggest challenge for education leaders isn't the technology — it's that AI adoption in schools is outpacing both teacher training and administrative policy. The institutions navigating this well have invested in faculty AI literacy before deploying tools, and have policies written for outcomes rather than processes.

### What's actually changing

- Khan Academy's Khanmigo AI tutor is live in 10,000+ schools; early efficacy studies show meaningful gains in math comprehension for students with 30+ minutes of weekly tutoring interaction
- AI writing feedback tools (Writable, Turnitin's AI features, EssayJack) are deployed in 20%+ of US high schools; the use case has shifted from detection to formative feedback
- The DOE's AI and the Future of Learning report (2023) established the framework all district AI policies reference; most state education AI guidance traces back to its principles
- 75%+ of college students report using AI tools for coursework despite honor code policies — most institutional policies are now shifting from prohibition to guided use
- AI-generated IEP drafts and differentiated lesson plans are saving special education teachers 5+ hours per week; this is the highest-impact administrative AI use case in K-12
- The ISTE (International Society for Technology in Education) AI in Education certification is becoming the baseline credential for EdTech coordinators and instructional technology leaders
