# 🏛️ Government & Public Sector — AI Resources

## AI & Your Industry

Federal, state, and local government agencies are deploying AI under a unique combination of pressures: public accountability requirements, civil rights obligations, procurement rules that favor incumbents, and an IT infrastructure that in many agencies dates to the 1970s. In 2026, the NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF) is the mandated governance standard for federal agency AI deployments. The OMB's M-23-22 memorandum requires agencies to identify AI use cases that affect rights or safety and apply additional oversight. CISA is actively publishing guidance on AI security. The DoD has its own AI adoption framework (CDAO) with separate classification levels for AI system risk. For public sector AI leaders, the challenge is threading the needle between the executive order mandating AI adoption and the congressional oversight scrutiny that follows every visible AI failure.

### What's actually changing

- The Executive Order on Safe, Secure, and Trustworthy AI (October 2023) and subsequent OMB guidance have made AI governance a statutory requirement for every federal agency — not an optional best practice
- GSA's AI.gov portal and the Federal AI Use Case Inventory catalog 1,000+ agency AI deployments; this database is the most comprehensive public record of how governments are actually using AI
- NIST AI RMF 1.0 is the mandatory governance framework for federal AI; AI Playbook supplements have been published for specific domains (healthcare, financial services, public sector)
- The IRS, SSA, and VA are deploying AI for fraud detection, benefits processing, and claims review at scale — but with oversight requirements that add 18–24 months to procurement cycles compared to commercial deployments
- CISA's 2025 AI Security Guidelines extended the Secure by Design framework to AI systems; federal contractors deploying AI must now meet minimum security posture requirements
- State-level AI legislation is accelerating — Colorado, Illinois, California, and Texas have all passed or are advancing AI use laws with government AI provisions; compliance fragmentation is the emerging challenge
