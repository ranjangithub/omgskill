# ⚖️ Legal, Compliance & Risk — AI Resources

## AI & Your Industry

Law and compliance are experiencing simultaneous disruption from two directions: AI is changing the work (document review, contract analysis, due diligence, regulatory research), and AI is becoming the subject of the work (EU AI Act enforcement, SEC AI guidance, ABA ethics opinions on AI, NIST AI RMF adoption). In 2026, contract review AI (Harvey, Ironclad, Kira, Luminance) is standard at large firms — junior associate document review hours have been materially reduced. The ABA's formal ethics opinion on generative AI use by lawyers (2024) established that competence obligations require understanding AI tools you use, not just results they produce. The compliance layer is where the most consequential AI work is happening: every enterprise deploying AI now needs an AI governance function, and the legal and compliance professionals who understand both the technology and the regulatory framework are in short supply.

### What's actually changing

- Harvey AI is deployed at Allen & Overy, PwC Legal, and 100+ law firms; contract review that took junior associates 8 hours now takes 30 minutes — the first-draft economics of legal work are permanently changed
- The EU AI Act became fully enforceable for General Purpose AI models in August 2025; any legal or compliance team advising clients on EU operations needs fluency in prohibited use cases, high-risk AI requirements, and transparency obligations
- SEC's 2025 AI guidance updates extend disclosure requirements to material AI risk — public companies must now assess and disclose whether AI model failures or regulatory action against AI tools represent material business risk
- NIST AI RMF 1.0 has been adopted as the baseline governance framework by 40%+ of Fortune 500 companies; legal teams are being asked to provide legal interpretation of its "Govern," "Map," "Measure," and "Manage" functions
- OWASP LLM Top 10 (prompt injection, training data poisoning, sensitive information disclosure) is becoming standard reference in AI incident response and cybersecurity legal frameworks
- E-discovery AI (Relativity, Nuix, Everlaw) has been used in federal court proceedings; the admissibility and methodology disclosure requirements for AI-assisted discovery are now active case law in multiple jurisdictions
