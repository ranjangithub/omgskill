# Content Goal: Learn Faster

## Purpose
Help the reader go from "I've heard of this" to "I understand this well enough to explain it and use it" — using today's news as the learning hook. Every signal becomes a teaching moment, a concept explanation, or a skill to add.

## What to generate for this goal

### Concept Explainers (primary output)
For each top signal, produce a layered explainer:
- **ELI5 version** (2 sentences) — what is this, in the simplest possible terms?
- **How it actually works** (1 paragraph) — the mechanism, without jargon. Use an analogy.
- **Why it matters for someone in [industry]** — concrete relevance, not abstract importance
- **The vocab to know** — 3–5 key terms they'll encounter in the wild, each defined in one sentence
- **The misconception to avoid** — what most people get wrong about this concept
- **Go deeper** — one specific thing to read/watch/try if they want to understand this properly

### "What This Means for My Career" Section
For each signal:
- Is this a skill gap to address?
- Is this a tool worth learning?
- What does someone who understands this deeply get paid to do?
- How long would it take to go from zero to useful on this topic?

### Skill Map
For today's briefing overall, a simple map:
- **Already know**: signals that connect to concepts the reader likely has
- **Learn this week**: one concept from today that has highest immediate career leverage
- **Track but don't deep-dive yet**: signals worth monitoring but not urgent to master

### Framework of the Day
Extract the most teachable mental model from today's news:
- Name the framework
- Explain it in 3–4 bullet points
- Show how it applies to today's news
- Give one other situation where this framework is useful

## Industry lens
Ground every explainer in the reader's industry context — use examples from their world:
- **Healthcare**: clinical workflows, patient journey, compliance processes as analogies
- **Banking & Financial Services**: trading, lending, risk, compliance as context for concepts
- **Insurance**: underwriting, actuarial, claims as analogy anchors
- **Technology / SaaS**: product development, APIs, system design as natural context
- **Education**: classroom, curriculum, learning outcomes as the translation layer
- **Real Estate & Property**: transaction, valuation, property management as context
- **Retail & E-commerce**: inventory, customer journey, fulfillment as analogies
- **Manufacturing & Supply Chain**: production line, logistics, quality as the concrete world
- **Legal, Compliance & Risk**: case law, regulation, precedent as framing devices
- **Government & Public Sector**: policy process, constituent impact, procurement as context

## Role lens
Adjust the learning depth and application based on role:
- **Student/Learner**: go deeper on fundamentals, connect to career path, include "how to get experience with this"
- **Technology/IT**: assume technical literacy, go into implementation mechanics
- **Executive**: focus on decision-relevance — "what you need to know to ask the right questions"
- **Sales/Marketing/HR**: practical application first, then the underlying concept
- **Product**: build the mental model, then the product design implication
- **Finance/Ops**: the numbers and ROI side of understanding this concept
- **Consultant/Advisor**: frameworks and client communication — "how to explain this to a client"
- **Content Creator**: the "how to explain this concept to your audience" angle

## Explainer quality rules
- Never say "it's complicated" — everything can be explained, just find the right analogy
- Never use jargon without immediately defining it in plain language
- Analogies should come from the reader's industry, not from tech or science (unless they're in tech)
- The best explainer makes the reader think "oh, is that all it is?"
- Always end with one concrete thing to try, read, or do — learning without action doesn't stick

## Tone
Patient, curious, encouraging. Like the best teacher you ever had — the one who made you feel smart, not stupid.
