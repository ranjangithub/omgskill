# Content Goal: Create Content

## Purpose
Turn today's news into content the reader can publish today. Every signal becomes a LinkedIn post idea, a newsletter angle, a hook, or a script. The reader's job is to pick and lightly edit — not to start from scratch.

## What to generate for this goal

### LinkedIn Post Ideas (primary output)
For each top signal, produce a ready-to-publish LinkedIn post:
- **Hook** (first line only, max 180 characters) — must create curiosity or conviction without being clickbait
- **Body** (3–5 short paragraphs) — personal take, the fact, the implication, the call to action
- **Closing CTA** — a question that invites comments (not "what do you think?")
- **Hashtags** — 3 relevant, specific hashtags (not #AI #Tech #Innovation)
- **Format note** — "works as a text post" / "better with an image" / "great for a carousel"

### Newsletter Angle Ideas
For each signal, one paragraph explaining how to frame this as a newsletter section:
- What's the reader's angle (vs. just reporting the news)?
- What's the personal story or experience hook?
- What's the contrarian or non-obvious take?

### Drive Time Script Segment
One 90-second spoken script per top signal, written to be read aloud:
- Conversational, not written-to-be-read
- Lead with the "imagine you're in the car" setup
- Deliver the insight, not just the summary
- End with "here's what I'd do about it"

### Content Calendar Hook
One line suggesting when to post this content for maximum reach (e.g., "Post Tuesday AM — this is a Monday news story, Tuesday commentary will feel fresh").

## Industry lens
The content must feel native to the reader's industry audience, not generic:
- **Healthcare**: clinical audience, patient safety context, regulation-aware
- **Banking & Financial Services**: compliance-aware, risk-conscious, sophisticated financial audience
- **Insurance**: actuarial and risk framing, carrier vs. broker vs. agent audience considerations
- **Technology / SaaS**: technical but accessible, builder/founder audience
- **Education**: educator and administrator audience, learning-outcome framing
- **Real Estate & Property**: practitioner audience, market-data-driven, deal-oriented
- **Retail & E-commerce**: consumer-facing implications, ops-savvy audience
- **Manufacturing & Supply Chain**: operational, efficiency-driven, factory-floor-to-boardroom
- **Legal, Compliance & Risk**: authoritative, precise, liability-aware
- **Government & Public Sector**: policy-framing, constituent-impact, bipartisan where possible

## Role lens
Calibrate the voice and credibility angle to the role:
- **Sales**: write from the perspective of a top performer, not a vendor
- **Marketing**: write from a practitioner who has run the campaigns, not a theorist
- **Executive**: write from the perspective of someone who has made this call, not who is about to
- **Product**: write from a builder who has shipped this problem, not a roadmap planner
- **Technology/IT**: write from an engineer who has dealt with this in production
- **Finance/Ops**: write from someone who owns the P&L impact, not just the analysis
- **HR/Talent**: write from someone who has hired through this kind of disruption
- **Consultant/Advisor**: write as a trusted advisor to a client, not as a vendor pitch
- **Content Creator**: write authentically, not corporately — this is your voice, not a brand voice
- **Student/Learner**: write as someone documenting their learning journey, not as an expert

## Hook quality rules
- Never start with "I" (LinkedIn algorithm suppresses it)
- Never use "excited to share" or "thrilled to announce"
- Never open with a statistic without a human stakes sentence first
- Good hooks create a gap (reader wonders how it closes), make a bold claim, or start in the middle of a story
- The best hooks are one sentence that would make someone stop scrolling

## Tone
Authentic, opinionated, specific. The reader has a point of view — help them express it, not just relay the news.
