# Content Goal: Sell Better

## Purpose
Give the reader intelligence that makes them more effective in every sales interaction today. Not generic sales tips — specific buyer intelligence derived from today's news that opens conversations, handles objections, and creates urgency.

## What to generate for this goal

### Buyer Signal Cards (primary output)
For each top signal, produce a "sales intel card":
- **The signal** — what happened (one sentence)
- **What your buyer is probably thinking** — the fear, hope, or question this creates in their mind
- **The conversation opener** — one question to start a call with ("I saw that [industry signal] — how is your team thinking about that?")
- **The pain point it surfaces** — which underlying problem this makes more urgent for buyers
- **The objection it creates** — what a buyer might say because of this news, and how to handle it
- **Urgency angle** — why this makes the timing of your outreach better right now

### Cold Outreach Templates
For the top 3 signals, a 3-sentence cold email/LinkedIn DM template:
- Line 1: The relevant industry signal (specific, not generic)
- Line 2: The pain this creates for their role specifically
- Line 3: The soft CTA (conversation, not demo)

### Objection Handler
Today's top news-driven objections and how to handle them:
- "We're waiting to see how this regulation shakes out" → response
- "Our budget is frozen because of [market condition]" → response
- "We're already working with [competitor who just launched X]" → response

### Discovery Questions (from today's news)
5 questions a rep can use in discovery calls today, derived from current industry signals — questions that make the buyer think "this person really knows my world."

## Industry lens
Every piece of intelligence must be grounded in the specific industry's buying dynamics:
- **Healthcare**: long sales cycles, committee buying, compliance veto points, clinical champion strategy
- **Banking & Financial Services**: risk committee approval, regulatory requirements as buying triggers, procurement complexity
- **Insurance**: carrier vs. MGA vs. broker dynamics, actuarial sign-off, compliance cycle timing
- **Technology / SaaS**: champion-driven, technical evaluation, build vs. buy narrative
- **Education**: procurement cycles (budget locked in spring), board approval, grant-funded buying
- **Real Estate & Property**: relationship-driven, fee structure sensitivity, market cycle timing
- **Retail & E-commerce**: seasonal buying cycles, margin pressure, speed-to-market urgency
- **Manufacturing & Supply Chain**: ROI-driven, long implementation cycles, plant-level champions
- **Legal, Compliance & Risk**: risk mitigation as primary buying motivation, partner-level sign-off
- **Government & Public Sector**: procurement rules, FISMA/FedRAMP requirements, long cycle, budget cycles

## Role lens
The reader is in sales — but calibrate to their specific sales motion:
- **Sales**: direct quota-carrying reps — give them conversation starters and objection handlers
- **Marketing**: sales-enablement content — give them the angles that support pipeline acceleration
- **Executive**: executive selling — board-level narratives, strategic urgency framing
- **Consultant/Advisor**: thought leadership that opens doors — insight-led business development
- All other roles: if they selected "Sell Better" as a goal, it likely means internal selling — frame around getting budget, buy-in, or stakeholder alignment

## Tone
Empathetic to the buyer, not manipulative. The goal is to be genuinely useful in a sales conversation — to show up as someone who understands the buyer's world. Not aggressive. Not pitchy. Consultative.

## What NOT to include
- Generic sales tips that don't connect to today's news
- Advice about how to "close" — this is about opening conversations, not closing deals
- Any framing that treats buyers as targets rather than people with real problems
