# Content Goal: Stay Updated

## Purpose
Deliver a crisp intelligence briefing so the reader walks into their day knowing what changed, what it means for their industry, and what everyone else will be talking about. No fluff. No filler.

## What to generate for this goal

### Signal Cards (primary output)
For each of the top 10 signals, produce:
- **Headline** — what happened, in plain language (one sentence)
- **Why it matters** — specific to the reader's industry and role (2–3 sentences)
- **Industry impact** — concrete consequences for their sector within 90 days
- **One line they can use** — a sentence they can drop into a Slack message, meeting, or email to sound informed

### Market Pulse (secondary output)
- 3–5 "quick hits" — short signals that don't need full analysis but are worth knowing
- Each quick hit: one bold fact + one-sentence implication

### The Week's Pattern
- One paragraph identifying the thread connecting today's top signals
- What macro shift does this week's news point toward?

## Industry lens
Frame every signal through the reader's specific industry vertical:
- **Healthcare**: patient outcomes, FDA/regulatory, EHR integration, clinical workflow
- **Banking & Financial Services**: risk, compliance (Basel, DORA, SR 11-7), client impact, market structure
- **Insurance**: underwriting, claims, actuarial models, regulatory (NAIC, state DOI)
- **Technology / SaaS**: product, competitive landscape, platform shifts, developer ecosystem
- **Education**: learning outcomes, institutional adoption, accreditation, student experience
- **Real Estate & Property**: transaction volume, PropTech tools, market data, regulatory zoning AI
- **Retail & E-commerce**: consumer behavior, supply chain, personalization, returns/fraud
- **Manufacturing & Supply Chain**: operational efficiency, predictive maintenance, logistics, compliance
- **Legal, Compliance & Risk**: enforcement actions, regulatory changes, liability, precedent
- **Government & Public Sector**: procurement, citizen services, security clearance, policy

## Role lens
Adjust the framing based on role:
- **Sales**: buyer behavior, competitive moves, pain points that open conversations
- **Marketing**: channel shifts, content opportunities, audience signals
- **Executive**: strategic implications, competitive threats, resource allocation signals
- **Product**: capability unlocks, user behavior, roadmap-relevant developments
- **Technology/IT**: tooling, architecture, security vulnerabilities, standards changes
- **Finance/Ops**: cost implications, efficiency gains, vendor pricing, process changes
- **HR/Talent**: workforce AI, hiring market, skills gap, culture implications
- **Consultant/Advisor**: client impact, frameworks, thought leadership angles
- **Content Creator**: trending topics, new angles, audience questions to answer
- **Student/Learner**: foundational context, career relevance, skills to prioritize

## Tone
Direct. No breathless adjectives. Trust the facts to be interesting. If a signal needs "game-changing" to sound important, it isn't.
