# StackDNA: Healthcare × Sales × Sell Better

## Who this person is

A sales professional in healthcare technology — selling to hospitals, health systems, payers, or pharma. Their quota depends on understanding what's changing in their buyer's world before the buyer does.

## What they need from every signal

1. **Buying trigger** — What event or news makes a decision-maker suddenly willing to buy?
2. **Conversation opener** — How do I open a cold call or email referencing this news?
3. **Pain point surfaced** — What problem does this news reveal that my solution solves?
4. **Objection handle** — If my prospect says "we're not ready," how does this news help me respond?

## How to frame signals for this persona

- Lead with the business impact on the buyer's P&L or compliance risk, not the technology
- Use CMS, FDA, or regulatory deadlines as urgency drivers
- Connect vendor news (Epic partnerships, CMS rules) to procurement conversations
- Show how competitors are moving — fear of being left behind is a real buying trigger
- Name the specific title of the person who should care: "Your CMIO is thinking about this"

## Sample enrichment for a healthcare sales professional

**Signal:** CMS finalizes AI bias audit requirements for MA utilization management

**Sales Intel:**
- **Buying trigger:** CMS deadline creates Q3 urgency for audit trail tooling
- **Target title:** VP Compliance, Chief Compliance Officer, IT Director at MA plans
- **Opener:** "I saw CMS published the final AI bias audit rule for MA utilization management — has your team mapped which of your tools fall under the documentation requirements?"
- **Pain point:** Most MA plans don't have audit trail infrastructure for their existing AI deployments. That's the gap.
- **Urgency:** Compliance deadline is Q1 next year. Procurement cycles mean you need to start now.

## Voice calibration

Healthcare sales professionals respond best to:
- Regulatory deadlines (creates urgency)
- Competitor movement (creates fear)
- Specific ROI numbers (creates business case)
- Patient outcome framing (creates mission alignment)

Avoid: pure technology framing, vendor feature lists, or forward-looking speculation
