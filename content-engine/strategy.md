# omgskill Curation Strategy

## Core principle

Signal-to-noise is the product. Every piece of content the user sees must pass one test:
**"Does this person need to know this today, given their job, industry, and goals?"**

If the answer is no, it gets cut. Not maybe — cut.

## The three lenses

Every signal is filtered through three lenses simultaneously:

1. **Industry lens** — Is this happening in my world, or adjacent to it?
2. **Role lens** — Does this change how I do my job this week?
3. **Goal lens** — Does this give me what I came for (content ideas / sales intel / learning / strategy)?

A signal that passes only 1 of 3 lenses is Noise. 2 of 3 is Quick Hit. All 3 is Top Signal.

## Classification system

| Class | Meaning | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Strategic Signal | Changes the game — affects your strategy this quarter | Top 3 of the day |
| Emerging Trend | Early signal of a direction that will matter in 6–18 months | Worth tracking |
| Tactical Update | Tool release, pricing change, workflow improvement | Use it now |
| Governance Alert | Regulatory, legal, or compliance development | Tell your team |
| Hype/Noise | Lots of attention, little substance | Label and skip |

## What we never include

- Press releases framed as news
- "AI startup raises $X" without a strategic implication
- Model benchmark comparisons without real-world workflow impact
- Conference announcements, award lists, or podcast promotion
- Year-in-review recaps, prediction lists, or trend roundups

## The expert voice

omgskill writes like a brilliant colleague, not a journalist. The expert voice:
- Names the implication before the event ("What this means: ...")
- Calls out what's hype vs. what's real
- Uses specific numbers (percentages, timelines, costs)
- Ends with an action, not a question

## Personalization depth

**Level 1 (Free):** Generic signals filtered by broad industry  
**Level 2 (Pro):** Signals filtered and framed by industry × role combination  
**Level 3 (Premium):** Signals filtered, framed, and personalized with the user's LinkedIn context, current projects, and voice preference
