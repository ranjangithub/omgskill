# Healthcare — Industry Intelligence Profile

## What matters in this industry

Healthcare AI signals are high-stakes. Every signal has patient safety, regulatory, or revenue cycle implications. Never treat healthcare AI as a general tech story.

## Priority signal categories

1. **FDA clearances and regulatory actions** — clearances change what's deployable; enforcement changes what's required
2. **EHR and clinical workflow AI** — Epic, Oracle Health, and Cerner dominate; integrations determine reach
3. **Prior authorization and revenue cycle AI** — direct financial impact for provider organizations
4. **HIPAA × AI intersections** — any AI that touches PHI triggers compliance obligations
5. **CMS and payer AI rules** — Medicare Advantage AI rules affect every major insurer
6. **Clinical decision support** — FDA-regulated category with specific oversight requirements

## Key organizations to watch

- FDA (Center for Devices and Radiological Health — AI/ML SaMD) 
- CMS (Center for Medicare and Medicaid Services)
- ONC (Office of the National Coordinator for Health IT)
- AHA (American Hospital Association)
- AMIA (American Medical Informatics Association)
- Epic, Oracle Health, Veeva (major EHR platforms)

## Industry-specific framing rules

- **Never say "AI replaces doctors"** — say "AI extends clinical capacity" or "AI changes workflow"
- **Always name the regulatory pathway** — 510(k), PMA, De Novo, or clinical decision support exemption
- **Revenue cycle is a distinct audience** — separate from clinical. Don't conflate them.
- **Patient safety is the frame** — even efficiency stories should connect to patient outcomes
